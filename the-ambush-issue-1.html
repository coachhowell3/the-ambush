From the Head Coach
Two weeks in. The foundation is being built in real time.

Tiger families and fans — welcome to the first issue of The Ambush. This newsletter exists for one reason: to keep you connected to what's happening inside this program, even when you can't see it firsthand.

We finished the Foundation Teaching Block this week — two weeks of voluntary workouts — and what I've seen has given me a lot to be excited about. Thirty-seven high school players showed up voluntarily. Twelve middle school players. We've averaged over 32 athletes per day across both weeks. Nobody required them to be here. They chose to be here.

In this program, that matters. We don't punish absence — we reward presence. Everything is earned: jersey numbers, locker assignments, your seat on the bus. The players who showed up these two weeks are already earning. Every rep counts.

We're just getting started. The best is ahead.

— Coach Howell  |  Hot Springs Tigers Football

Inside the Program
Meet the OVR App

This year we're using the Arena OVR App to track player development — and it's unlike anything most high school programs are doing. OVR measures the complete athlete: not just performance, but behavior, habits, and consistency.

The score is split 50/50 — half from measurable data like lifting numbers, 40 times, and GPA, and half from intangibles that coaches track daily: effort, toughness, leadership, and how a player represents the program.

What This Means for Your Athlete
Every day matters. Every rep in the weight room matters. How you treat your teammates matters. The OVR score is a live reflection of who you're becoming — not just what you can run. The app is currently available on the App Store, with Android / Google Play coming soon.
